Bulgogi

Description

Bulgogi is a beloved Korean dish that features thin slices of beef (often ribeye or sirloin) marinated in a savory-sweet mixture of soy sauce, sugar, garlic, sesame oil, and pear or apple for natural sweetness. The name "bulgogi" means "fire meat," a nod to its traditional preparation over an open flame or grill, although it's also commonly pan-fried. It's known for its tender texture, caramelized edges, and rich umami flavor with just a hint of sweetness.

Originating from Korea's Goguryeo era (circa 37 BCE), bulgogi has evolved from royal court cuisine to everyday comfort food. It became especially popular globally in the late 20th century as Korean food culture spread. Bulgogi is typically served with rice, lettuce leaves for wrapping, and a variety of banchan (side dishes), making it a balanced, satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 500g beef sirloin or ribeye, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp grated pear or apple (optional, for tenderness)
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 2 green onions, chopped

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, garlic, grated pear, black pepper, and sesame seeds to make the marinade.
  2. Add the sliced beef and onion to the marinade. Mix well and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (preferably a few hours).
  3. Heat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat and cook the beef slices in batches until browned and cooked through.
  4. Garnish with green onions and serve hot with steamed rice or lettuce leaves.
