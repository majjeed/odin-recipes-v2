Cheeseburger

The cheeseburger is one of the most iconic American foods, combining a seasoned ground beef patty with a slice of melted cheese, served in a soft bun. Toppings like lettuce, tomato, onions, pickles, and condiments such as ketchup or mustard are often added. The flavor is rich, juicy, and savory, with the cheese adding a creamy, salty element that complements the beef.

While hamburgers date back to the late 19th century, the cheeseburger rose to fame in the 1920s and 1930s in the U.S., with several diners and fast-food pioneers claiming credit for its invention. Today, it's a staple of American cuisine and has become a global symbol of comfort food and fast food culture.

Ingredients

  • 500g ground beef (80/20 lean/fat ratio)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 4 slices cheddar or American cheese
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Tomato slices
  • Onion slices
  • Pickles (optional)
  • Ketchup, mustard, or mayo (optional)
  • Butter or oil for cooking

Steps

  1. Form the ground beef into 4 patties and season both sides with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a skillet or grill over medium-high heat and cook the patties for 3–4 minutes on one side.
  3. Flip the patties, place a slice of cheese on top, and cook for another 3–4 minutes until the cheese melts and the beef is cooked to your liking.
  4. Toast the buns lightly, then assemble the burgers with lettuce, tomato, onions, pickles, and desired condiments.
