Pad Thai

Pad Thai is a stir-fried noodle dish that's widely recognized as Thailand's national dish. It features rice noodles sautéed with eggs, tofu or shrimp, bean sprouts, garlic, and peanuts, all tossed in a tangy, sweet-sour sauce made with tamarind paste, fish sauce, and palm sugar. The dish is often garnished with lime wedges and chili flakes, giving it a balance of salty, sweet, sour, and spicy flavors.

Though it feels ancient, Pad Thai was actually popularized in the 1930s and 1940s as part of a nation-building campaign in Thailand. It was promoted as a cheap, nutritious street food and quickly became a favorite throughout the country. Today, Pad Thai is one of the most internationally beloved Thai dishes, prized for its complexity and vibrant flavor.

Ingredients

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 2 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs
  • 100g tofu or shrimp
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup crushed peanuts
  • Lime wedges (for serving)
  • Chili flakes (optional)
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  1. Soak the rice noodles in warm water for 30 minutes or until softened, then drain.
  2. In a small bowl, mix tamarind paste, fish sauce, and sugar to create the sauce.
  3. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over medium heat. Sauté garlic and tofu (or shrimp) until golden.
  4. Push ingredients to one side, crack in the eggs, scramble them briefly, then mix everything together.
  5. Add the drained noodles and the sauce. Stir-fry until noodles are coated and slightly caramelized.
  6. Add bean sprouts and green onions, toss for another minute.
  7. Serve hot with crushed peanuts, lime wedges, and optional chili flakes.
