Pad Thai

Pad Thai is a stir-fried noodle dish that's widely recognized as Thailand's national dish. It features rice noodles saut√©ed with eggs, tofu or shrimp, bean sprouts, garlic, and peanuts, all tossed in a tangy, sweet-sour sauce made with tamarind paste, fish sauce, and palm sugar. The dish is often garnished with lime wedges and chili flakes, giving it a balance of salty, sweet, sour, and spicy flavors.

Though it feels ancient, Pad Thai was actually popularized in the 1930s and 1940s as part of a nation-building campaign in Thailand. It was promoted as a cheap, nutritious street food and quickly became a favorite throughout the country. Today, Pad Thai is one of the most internationally beloved Thai dishes, prized for its complexity and vibrant flavor.
