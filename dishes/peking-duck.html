Peking Duck

Peking Duck is a traditional Chinese dish known for its paper-thin, crispy skin and tender, flavorful meat. The duck is air-dried, glazed, and roasted until the skin becomes golden and crackling. It is typically served with thin pancakes, sliced scallions, cucumber, and hoisin sauce, allowing diners to assemble their own duck wraps.

This dish dates back to the imperial era of the Yuan Dynasty (around the 13th century) and gained legendary status during the Ming Dynasty. Originating from Beijing (formerly known as Peking), it remains one of China's most prestigious and ceremonial meals. Its rich, roasted aroma and indulgent flavor make it a standout on any menu.
