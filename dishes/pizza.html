Pizza

Pizza, especially the classic Margherita, originates from Naples, Italy, where it started as a simple flatbread topped with tomatoes, mozzarella cheese, and fresh basil. The combination of chewy crust, rich tomato sauce, and creamy cheese makes for a balanced and universally loved flavor profile. The Margherita, with its red, white, and green colors, is said to have been created in 1889 in honor of Queen Margherita of Savoy.

Though its roots are humble, pizza has become a global phenomenon, adapted in countless ways across cultures — from New York-style to deep-dish to gourmet varieties. Its versatility, affordability, and comforting taste have made it one of the most consumed dishes in the world.

Ingredients

  • 1 pizza dough ball (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1/2 cup crushed San Marzano tomatoes or tomato sauce
  • 200g fresh mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • All-purpose flour (for dusting)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to the highest temperature (ideally 250°C / 475°F) and place a pizza stone or baking sheet inside.
  2. On a floured surface, roll out the pizza dough to your desired thickness.
  3. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the base. Tear the mozzarella and scatter evenly.
  4. Add fresh basil leaves and drizzle with olive oil.
  5. Transfer the pizza to the hot oven and bake for 8–12 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.
  6. Slice and serve immediately.
