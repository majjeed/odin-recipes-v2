Ramen

Ramen is a Japanese noodle soup consisting of wheat noodles in a deeply flavored broth, often topped with sliced pork (chashu), soft-boiled egg, green onions, and nori (seaweed). There are several styles of broth — including shoyu (soy sauce), miso, tonkotsu (pork bone), and shio (salt) — each offering a different level of richness and depth. Ramen is known for its warm, comforting, umami-packed taste.

Though ramen has Chinese origins, it was transformed and embraced in Japan throughout the 20th century, becoming a cultural staple. It gained massive popularity after World War II and even more so with the invention of instant ramen in the 1950s. Today, ramen shops are a staple in Japan and in cities around the world.

Ingredients

  • 200g ramen noodles (fresh or dried)
  • 4 cups pork bone broth (or tonkotsu broth)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp miso paste (optional)
  • 2 soft-boiled eggs
  • 4 slices chashu pork (braised pork belly)
  • 1 sheet nori (seaweed), cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup sliced green onions
  • Bean sprouts (optional)
  • Sesame oil or chili oil (optional)

Steps

  1. Bring the pork bone broth to a simmer in a pot. Add soy sauce and miso paste (if using), stirring to combine.
  2. Cook ramen noodles in a separate pot according to package instructions. Drain and divide into bowls.
  3. Pour the hot broth over the noodles in each bowl.
  4. Top with chashu pork slices, soft-boiled egg halves, green onions, and nori.
  5. Add optional toppings like bean sprouts or chili oil as desired. Serve hot.
